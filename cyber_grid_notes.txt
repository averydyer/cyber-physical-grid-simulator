Power Substations:
- allow electricity to be transmitted securely and reliably
- can transmit electricity at different voltages
- one main role of substations is to convert electricity into different voltages
- voltage is stepped up or down through transformers
- substations are very similar to a post office, but for electricity
- think of a transformer like the different gear settings on a bike
- switches/circuit breakers allow electricians to do things such as
turning off the power to certain lines so they can perform repairs,
or reroute the electricity if there is a problem somewhere
- if lightning strikes a power line, circuit breakers will turn
off the electricity flow to protect the transformers and other equipment
- insulators stop electricity from leaving the wires and entering
the metal support structures or the ground

Transmission Substations:
- found often where electricity enters the network, near major power sources
- also found where it leaves the transmission network for distribution to homes/businesses

- electricity is routed from the transmission system into a distribution
substation via a GSP (grid supply point)

Distribution Substations:
- allows electricity to enter homes/businesses
- distribution networks are made of smaller overhead lines

What is a substation in my model?
Attributes:
- transformers to change voltage
- circuit breakers to cut electricity to certain lines
Behaviors:
- transmission substations receive electricity from power plant 
and lower the voltage
- distribution substations lower voltage and allow electricity
to be distributed to neighborhoods/businesses
- grid supply point substation moves electricity from the transmission
system into a distribution system
- electricity is cut off from any damaged lines by circuit breakers
What could go wrong:
- environmental damage (storm, lightning, etc) to power lines or substation
- cyber attack (focus on this one the most) leading to mass blackout
- physical attack of equipment
- erosion of equipment over time
Capacity/current load:
- substation should track current load and be able to readjust if
certain regions are requiring more energy than usual
- different substations will handle different energy amounts based
on their role (transmission, distribution, etc)
Shut down/recovery:
- should be able to automatically shut down power to any damaged lines
- should be able to deal with receiving different amounts of 
energy from power plant

Regions/demand nodes
Power plant: supplies the power to surrounding area
City: requires higher amount of energy, power outage is more severe.
Would power outage affect transportation such at city lines/metro?
Neighborhood: requires smaller amount of energy, power outage has
least negative repercussions here
Data center: AI data centers require a huge amount of energy
Water treatment plant: very negative impact if water treatment plant
loses power. Can it operate without power? This could be near/within
the city and provide clean drinking water to inhabitants

Control Logic
- load should be reassigned automatically when needed
- overload should be detected
- should act automatically

Project breakdown:
1 power plant, supplies energy
3 (maybe more) substations: 1 transmission, 1 GSP, 1 distribution
4 (maybe more) regions: neighborhood, city, data center, water treatment plant
- several regions can be connected to one substation, however it must
be organized in a way so that regions requiring more energy receive
it from substations with bigger capacities

Simulation substation:
- a node that distributes power and enforces capacity limits
Good attributes for the substation class:
- name
- capacity (max power it can supply)
- current load
- status (online/offline)
- connected regions

What could go wrong?
- substation shutdown (cyber command injection, breaker manipulation, etc)
- false telemetry (controller thinks load is normal when it's not)
- delayed response (controller fails to rebalance load in time)

Regions
City - Demand = high, Criticality = high
Data center - Demand = very high, Criticality = medium
Water treatment plant - Demand = medium, Criticality = very high
Suburban neighborhood - Demand = low, Criticality = low

System:
Grid controller - substations (3) - regions (4)

Connection model:
- substations will connect to multiple different regions so that
cascading overload/failures can be simulated.
- maybe lower demand nodes like the neighborhood will only be connected
to one substation, but higher capacity substations are connected
to multiple, higher demand nodes
- load redistribution is possible if one node demands more/less
energy than usual

Rough Power Demand Values:
City: 80 MW
Neighborhood: 25 MW
Data Center: 120 MW
Water Treatment: 40 MW

Total demand = 265 MW
total supply = 315 MW
reserve capacity = 50 MW (19%)

S1: 140 MW (supplies to data center, city, water treatment)
S2: 100 MW (supplies to data center, city)
S3: 80 MW (supplies to neighborhood, water treatment)

Data center: S1, S2
City: S1, S2
Water treatment: S1, S3
Neighborhood: S3
- if load is greater than substation capacity, overload occurs
- if S1 failed, there would be a power outage in the city. The city's
80 MW might go to S2 and cause an overload

- equal split load model is used (ex. S1 supplies 60 MW to data center,
S2 supplies 60 MW to data center. If one substation fails, the remaining
one takes the full load)
